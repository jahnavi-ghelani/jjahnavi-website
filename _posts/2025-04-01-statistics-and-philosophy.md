The road to conclusions is paved with innumerable decisions. To list a few: 
* ­ЪћЇ selecting the analysis approach
* ­ЪћЇ designing the feature set - variables, aggregation levels, handling inter-feature relations
* ­ЪћЇ whether to scale/normalize/weigh features & how 
* ­ЪћЇ how to effectively validate results
* ­ЪћЇ identifying impactful improvements 
* ­ЪћЇ how to future-proof the model to update with new data <br>
& so on.  

Each decision branches into more choices, but you get the idea.

I discovered two brilliant terms that Box aptly calls "pathologies": 
* ­ЪД« **Cookbookery**: mindless application of statistical techniques - a plague in today's data science community. 
* ­ЪД« **Mathemastery**: theory for theory's sake without practical application. 

Theory and practice must always inform each other for genuine progress.
